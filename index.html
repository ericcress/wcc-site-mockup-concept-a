330 G Street · West Sacramento

A village at 330 G Street.

Forty households share a 5,800-square-foot common house on the riverside in West Sacramento. The dining room fills up most weeks — movie nights, shared dinners someone offered to host, workshops residents organized among themselves. Residents started as strangers a few years ago. Now they share tomatoes from the garden and know whose week was hard.

Life at Washington Commons

Most of what makes Washington Commons work happens outside the unit.

Mornings in the garden

Bea waters the native garden most mornings before the heat lifts. Willa heads the landscaping team and can name the 35 plant species on the property. The community calendar fills itself around the seasons.

The common house

5,800 square feet of shared kitchen, dining, guest rooms, craft, and exercise space. Friday Snack & Schmooze most weeks. Sunday Movie Night. Potluck brunches and dinners that alternate. The grandkids stay in the guest suite when they visit.

The neighborhood

Two blocks to the Sacramento River. Walkable to the West Sacramento farmers market. Ten minutes to downtown Sacramento, fifteen to the Amtrak, twenty to the airport.
