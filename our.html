Outside the Door

Two blocks from the river.
Ten minutes to downtown.

Washington Commons is at 330 G Street in West Sacramento — on a quiet residential block, two blocks from the Sacramento River, four blocks from the Tower Bridge into downtown Sacramento. The neighborhood is older West Sac: walkable, low-rise, the kind of place where you know the coffee shop owner after a month.

A Short Walk Away

Five minutes on foot.

Residents walk to most of these without thinking. The garage stays full of bikes for the longer ones.

01

Sacramento River + the Riverwalk

Two blocks. Bird walks Bea leads in spring. The path runs all the way to downtown. Janet's pickleball games happen at Sutter Health Park, also two blocks.

02

Drake's The Barn

Local brewery, casual food, outdoor tables. A common Friday spillover after Snack & Schmooze.

03

West Sacramento Farmers Market

Wednesdays through summer, a short walk from the door. The garden at WC is a hobby; the farmers market is the staples.

04

Sutter Health Park

River Cats AAA baseball, soon the Oakland A's. Two blocks. A summer evening that costs the price of a ticket.

Ten Minutes Away

Across the bridge.

Across the Tower Bridge is downtown Sacramento — the museums, the Golden 1 Center, the Amtrak station with 11 trains a day to the Bay Area, dozens of restaurants. Residents go for the museums, the music, the kids' games, the late dinners.

  • Old Sacramento — restaurants, the rail museum, the riverfront
  • Golden 1 Center — Kings basketball, concerts
  • The Crocker Art Museum
  • Sacramento Amtrak — 11 trains daily to the Bay Area
  • Sacramento International Airport — 20 minutes
How Residents Use It

What the neighborhood is for.

Daily walks

Lydia walks M'Chaiah along the Riverwalk most mornings. Anne walks faster — she founded WALKSacramento. The block is flat and the river is two minutes away.

Weekly stops

The farmers market, the coffee at the corner, the pickleball game, the brewery for the Friday spillover. Routes that didn't need a car twenty years ago and don't now.

Day trips

The Amtrak to the Bay every couple of weeks. The Crocker on a Sunday. A wine drive into the Delta. The airport in twenty minutes for the longer trips. Family in for the holidays — guest rooms at the common house, ten minutes from the train.

Walk it yourself.

Tours start in the common house and end with a walk around the block. About an hour.
