Life at Washington Commons

Most of what makes Washington Commons work happens outside the unit.

Bea waters the native garden most mornings, before the heat lifts. The common house has guest rooms — when residents' grandkids come, they stay in their own quiet wing rather than crashing the couch.

The calendar fills itself. Movie nights. Shared dinners when someone wants to host. Workshops, gatherings, things residents organize among themselves. The craft room and the exercise space get steady use. There's a dog wash, too.

It's a small community on purpose. Around forty households. Walkable to the West Sacramento farmers market, ten minutes to downtown Sacramento, fifteen to the train.
